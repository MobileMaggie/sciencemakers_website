ScienceMakers at Mozfest

Ravensbourne College, Saturday , 7 November noon- 1 pm

We'll be at Mozfest2015 again this year, running a workshop on building an Open IoT Stack for Schools.

There's been a lot going on lately in the Internet of Things, especially in the area of education.

The BBC Microbit is of course the biggest change.

We had a chance to try one out at the Over the Air preview and we're sure it will be a game changer. The Microbit people have built Microbit with capacity for IoT. And the web-based programming interface is a real star.

There are also many IoT initiatives for schools, including, for the UK, the Internet of School Things.

So many, in fact, that we're finding it hard to choose. So we thought we'd start a discussion, to lay out a basic framework for an IoT stack and fill in some of the boxes (with choices and ideas about what we'd like to see).

For those of you who've attended our past workshops, this is a bit of a departure for us. It will be more of a community discussion and less of a hands-on event.

Everyone is welcome to attend, whether you're a scientist, a developer, an educator, an IoT enthusiast, , or just curious.

To get things rolling, we've started an etherpad. We'll be sharing our thoughts there in the days leading up to the workshop and we'd encourage you to do so too, if you feel like contributing.
