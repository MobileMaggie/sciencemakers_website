Barrow ScienceMakers: Arctic Citizen Science

On the shores of the Arctic ocean, climate change is also changing how subsistence communities hunt for food. Rapidly decreasing ice cover makes hunting perilous and up-to-the-minute information about ice thickness is becoming a matter of life or death.

We are working with local communities and students to develop mobile and sensor technology which will help their hunters to identify change and share their knowledge. Our hope is that this technology will make a difference both to these communities and to our understanding of the effects of climate change.

In July 2014, we headed up to Alaska for another ScienceMakers hack, where we worked with STEM students to develop some sensor and mobile data collection projects.

Since then, we've been working to develop some of the student projects from the hack, including a mobile app for animal indentification.
