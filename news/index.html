ScienceMakers is a global community for sharing and collaborating on hands-on Science projects. Our online base is http://www.sciencemakers.net/, but this is where we get together in person. Do you enjoy tinkering with software, hardware, sensors, classical science tools - even play dough or LEGO? Then come join like-minded folks in your community at the local ScienceMakers Club - or even start your own! For questions or support, contact us via https://twitter.com/SciMakers.

Barrow ScienceMakers

On the shores of the Arctic ocean, climate change is also changing how subsistence communities hunt for food. Rapidly decreasing ice cover makes hunting perilous and up-to-the-minute information about ice thickness is becoming a matter of life or death.

We are working with the Yupik and Inupiaq to develop mobile and sensor technology which will help their hunters to identify change and share their knowledge. Our hope is that this technology will make a difference both to these communities and to our understanding of the effects of climate change.

In May, we ran a ScienceMakers hack at Science Museum LATES, where we tried out some of the same basic methods for DIY science hacking that we’ll be using in Alaska. We wired up a few arduino boards with sensors and showed people how to share the information they generated.

In July, we'll be heading up to Alaska for another ScienceMakers hack, where we'll be working with STEM students to develop some sensor and mobile data collection projects.

This ScienceMakers project is a collaboration with Diana Mastracci at UCL Excites and the Citizen Cyberlab project

Find out more about Diana's project.

Science Museum Lates Hands-On Science Hack
